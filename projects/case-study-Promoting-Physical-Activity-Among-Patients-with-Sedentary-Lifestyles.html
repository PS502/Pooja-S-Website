Promoting Physical Activity Among Patients with Sedentary Lifestyles

Designing a behavioral science intervention to drive daily movement and habit formation via wearable technology.

Challenge

Many patients with sedentary lifestyles opt for screen time and inactive behaviors, leading to adverse health effects such as an increased risk of heart disease, stroke, and weight gain.

Project Overview

I designed an intervention to encourage physical activity among patients with a history of heart disease and sedentary habits for a line of wireless-enabled wearable technology—primarily physical fitness monitors and activity trackers.

Strategy & Execution

1. Identifying Key Behavior

My research identified that users often struggle with maintaining consistent exercise habits due to a lack of motivation and accountability. Behavioral science suggests individuals are more likely to adhere to exercise routines when supported by social accountability, competition, and tangible goal-setting.

Because setting and achieving daily step goals proved to be the key behavior driving user engagement, we introduced “Challenges”—a feature designed to help users maintain consistent movement by participating in structured, goal-oriented challenges.

2. Segmentation Based on Motivators

Users were categorized into intrinsic or extrinsic motivational profiles based on their personal values:

  • Extrinsic Motivators: Individuals driven by competition, rankings, and peer recognition were grouped together.
  • Intrinsic Motivators: Individuals driven by personal achievement, health longevity, or family connections were grouped separately.

3. Crafting Messaging & Positioning

“Challenges” was positioned as a fun, motivating way to stay active and connect with friends, family, and fellow users. Tailored text messages were delivered based on each user's motivational profile:

Extrinsic Motivator Trigger:
“You're just 2,000 steps away from breaking your group’s record. That’s just 10 minutes of walking to reach the top of the leaderboard!”
Intrinsic Motivator Trigger:
“A small stroll today means more playtime with your granddaughter tomorrow!”

4. Incentives, Rewards & UX Optimization

  • Gamification Mechanics: Users earned badges, virtual trophies, and milestone rewards for completing “Challenges.”
  • Frictionless UX: Simplified joining flows and real-time activity feedback made progress transparent and rewarding.

Impact & Results

+4,000 Daily Steps Within one month, patients increased daily physical activity (primarily walking) by an average of 4,000 steps.
81% Habit Formation Rate At the 3-month mark, 81% of patients had formed a sustained habit of regular physical activity.
+50% Activity Duration Increase Approximately 20% of habit-formers voluntarily increased their daily physical activity duration from 30 minutes to 45 minutes.

Key Takeaway

By combining behavioral science insights with a targeted product marketing strategy, this feature drove sustained user engagement, fostered community, and empowered patients to achieve lasting health milestones.
